Algorithmic Redistricting using MCMC Methods

In the Information Age, political polarization has reached levels unprecedented in American history. We present a redistricting algorithm powered by Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) to provide an unbiased and transparent way to represent voters.

What Is Redistricting?

Redistricting is the process of drawing electoral district boundaries under specific state-mandated guildelines. This occurs around every ten years with the census collection, but may occur more frequently.

Necessity for Redistricting Algorithms

There are many current cases where human bias affects the legitimacy of redistricting plans, especially concerning gerrymandering. In Alabama, there was a case in 2022 where the proposed district map infringed on the Voters Rights Act, as while Alabama has a 27% black population, only 1 of 7 proposed districts would be majority-Black.

The Efficiency Gap

The efficiency gap is a useful metric for "quantifying" fairness. If we consider wasted votes being votes for the winner in excess and unused votes for the losing candidate, we can generate an equation to determine the efficiency of a district's votes. If we take (wasted votes for the loser) - (wasted votes for the winner) and divide by total votes, we can generate a value to determine the competitiveness of a district.
